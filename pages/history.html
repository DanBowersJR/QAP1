First generation (1953-1962)

The first generation Corvette, launched in 1953 as a two-seater sports car, featured Harley Earl's pioneering fiberglass body. Initially powered by a six-cylinder "Blue Flame" engine, performance improved significantly in 1955 with the optional 265 cubic inch V8. In 1957, the Corvette introduced a 283 cubic inch V8, including the "Fuelie" model, which delivered one horsepower per cubic inch. This generation, produced until 1962, set the foundation for the Corvette's future success.

Second generation (1963-1967)

The second-generation Corvette, introduced in 1963, featured a modern, aggressive design and was offered as both a convertible and coupe. Powered by a big-block V8, it reached new performance heights with the L88 engine, officially rated at 430 horsepower but delivering much more. This marked a significant advancement in Corvette history.

Third generation (1968-1982)

The third-generation Corvette, or C3, debuted in 1968 and was produced until 1982. It featured a more aerodynamic design with various body styles, including a T-top and hatchback. The L88 engine option, regarded as the "holy grail" of C3 production, was carried over from the previous generation.

Fourth generation (1984-1986)

The fourth-generation Corvette, or C4, was introduced in 1984 and produced until 1996. It featured a modern design and a range of V8 engines. Notably, the ZR-1 model used a DOHC all-aluminum LT-5 engine developed by Lotus, making it the only Corvette with a non-Chevrolet engine.

Fifth generation (1997-2004)

The first-generation Corvette, introduced in 1953, was a two-seater with Harley Earl's innovative fiberglass body. Initially powered by a six-cylinder "Blue Flame," it gained a significant performance boost in 1955 with an optional 265 cubic inch V8. By 1957, the Corvette offered a 283 cubic inch V8, including the high-performance "Fuelie" model. Produced until 1962, this generation laid the groundwork for the Corvette's future success.

Sixth generation (2005-2013)

The sixth-generation Corvette, or C6, launched in 2005 and produced until 2013, featured an aggressive design in coupe and convertible styles. It included the high-performance Z06 and ZR1 models, with the ZR1 boasting a powerful 638 horsepower engine.

Seventh generation (2014-2019)

The seventh-generation Corvette, or C7, launched in 2014 and produced until 2019, featured a sleek design in coupe and convertible styles. The Z06 and ZR1 models returned, with the ZR1 equipped with a 755 horsepower V8 and 715 lb-ft of torque for enhanced performance.

Eighth generation (2020-present)

The eighth-generation Corvette, or C8, introduced in 2020, is the first mid-engine model with a bold, futuristic design. The Z06 variant delivers 670 horsepower, enhancing its driving excitement. This mid-engine layout positions the C8 alongside other exotic cars, boosting its appeal and value.
